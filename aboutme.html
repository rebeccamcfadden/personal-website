About Me:


I am a sophomore at Texas A&M majoring in computer science with a minor in embedded system design. I am an officer for the A&M branch of ACM-W, Aggie Women in Computer Science (AWiCS) and I am involved in many other students groups on campus, such as Dance Arts Society and Hullabaloo Band.


Outside of school, I enjoy painting, reading, cooking/baking, dance, and tinkering with Raspberry Pis. Though all of those are generally indoor activities, I like to spend a fair amount of time outdoors, whether that be running, hiking, or just sitting by the pool at my apartment.


After I graduate, I hope to move back to my home area of Washington D.C. or along the East Coast to spend more time with my three siblings and my family. While I don't have a specific company or focus area in computer science that I'd like to pursue at this time, I hope to work on a project or team that showcases the "magic" of technology to the world. I came to computer science from aerospace/mechanical engineering because I was inspired at a young age by theme park attraction design and animatronics. As I got older, that fascination found a new subject in the form of space travel after having witnessed the final fly-in of the Discovery space shuttle to Washington Dulles Airport. Yet when I started taking college classes, I found my interest pulled by my computer science minor classes and I eventually made the switch. Therefore, my goal is to find a job that combines my inspirations with the field I enjoy.
